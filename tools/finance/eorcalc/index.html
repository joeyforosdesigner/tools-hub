How to Use EOR Cost Calculator

Planning to expand your team internationally requires precise financial planning to avoid unexpected expenses. Our EOR Cost Calculator is designed to help HR managers, business owners, and finance teams estimate the true cost of employment in over 100 countries. To get started, simply enter the gross annual salary you intend to offer your candidate. This figure serves as the baseline for all subsequent calculations regarding taxes and benefits.

Next, select the target country where your new employee will be located. Local labor laws vary significantly, and this selection allows the tool to pull accurate data for social security contributions, health insurance mandates, and other statutory benefits specific to that jurisdiction. Additionally, choose the contract type, such as full-time or part-time, as this can influence the percentage of employer contributions required by local law.

Once you click 'Calculate', the tool provides a comprehensive breakdown of the costs. You will see the Employer of Record (EOR) management fee, which covers payroll processing, compliance, and local HR administration. The calculator also details the employer's share of taxes and social contributions, clearly distinguishing them from the employee's net pay. By reviewing the total monthly and annual cost, you can make informed budgeting decisions and compare the financial implications of hiring in different regions before signing any contracts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate the total cost of an employee using an Employer of Record?

Enter the gross salary and select the country to instantly see a breakdown of employer taxes, social contributions, and EOR fees.

What fees are typically included in an Employer of Record pricing model?

EOR fees usually include a monthly management fee per employee covering payroll, compliance, and onboarding, plus statutory employer contributions.

Does the EOR Cost Calculator include local social security and tax contributions?

Yes, the tool automatically calculates mandatory employer social security contributions and local taxes based on the selected country's legislation.

Why is the total cost of employment higher than the gross salary?

The total cost includes the gross salary plus mandatory employer benefits, social security taxes, and the administrative fee charged by the EOR provider.

Can I compare hiring costs between different countries with this tool?

Absolutely, by changing the target country field, you can instantly compare how employer contributions and EOR fees vary across different jurisdictions.

How does the contract type affect the Employer of Record costs?

Contract types may influence the applicable tax rates and benefit requirements, which the calculator adjusts to provide an accurate cost estimate.

Is this calculator suitable for budgeting for remote international employees?

Yes, it provides a precise estimate of annual and monthly expenses, helping you budget accurately for remote teams without setting up a local entity.

What is the difference between gross salary and total cost to company?

Gross salary is the employee's earnings before tax, while total cost to company includes that salary plus all employer taxes, benefits, and EOR service fees.
